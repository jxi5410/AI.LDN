Investor Concentration

London AI investment is concentrated among a small number of global and European funds. SoftBank appears in Wayve, Graphcore, and nScale. Accel backs Helsing, Synthesia, and others. Lightspeed appears in Anthropic and multiple growth rounds. The most active London-native VCs are Balderton Capital, LocalGlobe, Seedcamp, and MMC Ventures — each with portfolios spanning the ecosystem.

Market Context

NatWest's Future of UK Innovation report (March 2026) found that AI startups raised over £6 billion in 2025 — more than a third of all UK venture capital. The UK added five new unicorns in 2025 (Isomorphic Labs, MUBI, Navys, Tide, Nothing), bringing the total to 29 worth £145 billion. Beauhurst reports that London now hosts over 6,030 active AI companies, up 523% since 2022.

What the Funding Data Reveals

Three patterns emerge from the data. First, round sizes are bimodal: a small number of mega-rounds ($500M+) coexist with many seed and Series A rounds under $30M. Second, sovereign AI is a new capital category — nScale's $2B round was driven by government interest in in-jurisdiction compute. Third, biotech AI valuations have corrected sharply — BenevolentAI and Exscientia both traded at steep discounts to their peak valuations, reflecting the market's realisation that AI speeds discovery but doesn't eliminate clinical risk.
